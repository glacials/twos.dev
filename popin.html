the remote pop-in

this page describes a method of dealing with one of the missing features of fully remote teams: the pop-in.

the pop-in

the pop-in, back before open floorplans, is when you physically walk into someone's office to ask them a question or spitball an idea. in an open floorplan it is swiveling your chair or walking to their desk.

the pop-in is valuable because it's as informal as email, but as high-bandwidth and low-latency as meetings. it's interruptive to the pop-innee, but that interruption power is self-limiting: the effort of physically going somewhere is high, and social dynamics can add cost if, say, the pop-innee isn't having a good day.

slack dms attempted to replace the pop-in, but they reduced the effort needed of the pop-inner too much. the reduction in effort leads to more interruptions (a little lower in intensity, but a lot higher in number) and even to the replacement of better-suited mediums like trackers, emails, and wikis with slack dms.

there's a lot wrong with pop-ins, but like any tool they have their uses. many great products—companies, even—have been built on the shoulders of the pop-in.

The Remote Pop-in

There's really no remote equivalent. There are some products that help you "hang out" like facebook portal, but nothing addressed the pop-in.

at yourbase we decided we needed one. rather than build another tool, we experimented with discord. i'm happy to say we found something that worked, and even solves the worst problem of open-office pop-ins—the inability to shut your damned door.

The Setup

In our company Discord server, we give everyone one voice channel of their own. This is your "office". When you are online and able to be disturbed, you join your office, alone. You stay in there as long as you are "in".

Because of how Discord works, everyone can see who is in what office at all times. but you can only hear conversations for the room you're in. Joining a room plays an obvious audio chime and is not something you can do sneakily.

When someone wants to pop-in, they join your voice channel. The chime plays, they say hello, you unmute and turn on video if you like, and have your pop-in. And that's really it.

If you're going to the restroom and will be AFK, you can join the restroom channel. You're in, but away. No one is actually wearing a headset on the toilet, it's just a fun way to feel like a real office.

We've even accidentally fallen into some typical office behaviors:

  • we've started having meetings in people's offices just because it's easier
  • we have a "cafeteria" room where you can eat your lunch if you've got nothing going on irl
  • we have a "rooftop" and other common spaces where some people chill, which signals that they're open for a chat

The oddest one is that when it's slow around the office, you can tell. And you can almost hear how quiet it is when you're the first one in or the last one out.

this system has worked well for us, and we didn't have to build any more tools. thanks to discord for making such a flexible platform.
